Mnemba Island Snorkeling

Swim with dolphins and explore Africa's premier marine reserve

Half Day (5 Hours) 2-12 People 4.9 (220+ Reviews)

Tour Overview

Mnemba Atoll is a tiny private island surrounded by one of the most spectacular coral reefs in the Indian Ocean. This protected marine conservation area is home to an incredible diversity of marine life, making it one of Africa's premier snorkeling destinations.

Your adventure begins with an early morning departure to catch the dolphins that frequent these waters. Watch for pods of bottlenose and spinner dolphins as they play in the waves â€“ if conditions are right, you may even have the chance to swim alongside these magnificent creatures.

The main attraction is the stunning coral reef that surrounds Mnemba Atoll. The crystal-clear waters (visibility often exceeds 30 meters) reveal a kaleidoscope of colorful coral formations, tropical fish, sea turtles, rays, and countless other marine species. Even experienced snorkelers are amazed by the biodiversity here.

Note: While Mnemba Island itself is private (home to an exclusive resort), the surrounding waters are open to all, and we'll snorkel in the best spots around the atoll's perimeter.

Tour Itinerary

  • 6:00 AM - Hotel Pickup

    Early start for the best dolphin sighting chances

  • 7:00 AM - Departure from Nungwi/Matemwe

    Board the boat and head to Mnemba waters

  • 7:30 AM - Dolphin Spotting

    Search for dolphins and opportunity to swim with them

  • 8:30 AM - First Snorkeling Session

    Explore the vibrant coral reefs

  • 9:30 AM - Break & Refreshments

    Rest on the boat with snacks and drinks

  • 10:00 AM - Second Snorkeling Session

    Explore a different section of the reef

  • 11:00 AM - Return Journey

    Head back to shore

  • 12:00 PM - Hotel Drop-off

    Return to your accommodation

    • Sea turtles (green and hawksbill)
    • Colorful reef fish (angelfish, parrotfish, butterflyfish)
    • Moray eels and octopus
    • Eagle rays and stingrays
    • Spectacular coral formations
