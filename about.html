Destination Guide

About Zanzibar

Zanzibar, a semi-autonomous archipelago off the coast of Tanzania, is one of Africa's most captivating destinations. Known as the "Spice Island," this tropical paradise has been a crossroads of African, Arab, Persian, Indian, and European cultures for centuries.

The main island, Unguja (commonly called Zanzibar), offers an enchanting blend of pristine white-sand beaches, turquoise waters, historic Stone Town (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and lush spice plantations. The smaller island of Pemba is known for its untouched coral reefs and clove production.

Beyond its natural beauty, Zanzibar captivates visitors with its rich history, warm hospitality, and unique Swahili culture. From the narrow winding streets of Stone Town to the peaceful fishing villages, every corner tells a story.
